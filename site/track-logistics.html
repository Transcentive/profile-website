Module 03.1

03.1Structural Freight Engineering

High-volume trailer work at UPS, where the load is the structure. Diverse inventory — standard parcels to oversized freight — is read as a packing problem: maximize cubic space while keeping the stack standing through a full transit. The discipline is the same as any architecture, only the material is cardboard and the deadline is the next sort.

  • The Technical Load. High-volume trailer management across mixed inventory — standard parcels through oversized freight.
  • The “Wall” Method. Systematic package walls that maximize cubic fill while reinforcing the stack for height and strength.
  • Transit Integrity. Engineering the load for safety and optimization inside high-tempo hub operations.
  • Operational Collaboration. Interfacing with sorters and supervisors to keep system flow uninterrupted.

Module 03.2

03.2Last-Mile Velocity

With Geneship on the Amazon last mile, the problem inverts: the wall comes apart in the exact order it has to leave the van. Delivery is engineered backward from the route — the load sequenced at the depot so each stop is a single reach, not a search. Velocity is what's left when the friction has been packed out in advance.

  • Strategic Sorting. Pre-delivery van-loading logic built for route efficiency and stop-by-stop velocity.
  • Adherence & Execution. A quick, sustained pace while honoring precise customer delivery instructions.
  • Navigational Systems. Expert operation of delivery vehicles and routing through dense, complex stop lists.
  • Real-Time Communication. Managing the digital and voice channels between dispatch and the customer.
